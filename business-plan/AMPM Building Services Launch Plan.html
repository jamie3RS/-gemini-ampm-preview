Section 01 · Executive summary

Incorporated. Ready to trade. Pipeline on day one.

AMPM Building Services Limited is an incorporated subsidiary of AMPM Group Limited, launching as a full building services contractor across M&E, fit-out, roofing and groundworks. The strategy is phased: sub-contract into Gemini AMPM from month one; approach friendly clients directly from month two. Year-one revenue target: £4 million.

The entity is designed to capture a large volume of building-services work currently sub-contracted out of Gemini AMPM to third parties — bringing it in-house, on a separate P&L, with a clean risk profile. In Phase 1, Building Services sub-contracts into Gemini on existing projects, generating immediate revenue with minimal new-business risk. In Phase 2, the entity approaches friendly clients directly, establishing its own client relationships and order book. Both phases run in parallel; Phase 2 begins as soon as insurance and accreditations allow.

The structure deliberately de-risks Gemini AMPM. New supply-chain relationships, new client contracts, and the operational risks inherent in a growing business entity are held within the new company rather than consolidated onto Gemini's P&L and accreditation register. Gemini retains its 27-year track record clean.

Why this is the right next move

  • Captive pipeline from day one via Gemini's existing project order book — no new-business development required to start trading
  • Operationally ready: the Group's back-office, finance, IT and HR functions already exist, keeping fixed cost low
  • Tier-1 and institutional clients already known to the management team are actively procuring building-services work
  • Sector tailwinds: HVAC retrofits, heat-pump installations, EV charging, Building Safety Act remediation
  • Clean entity start — no legacy bad contracts; margin discipline from day one
  • Fastest organic growth lever the Group has; reduces the number of acquisitions required to hit the £25–30m Group target

Decision required

The decision before the Board is not whether to launch. AMPM Building Services Limited is already incorporated and the commercial case is clear. The decision is how quickly to execute the launch checklist — placing insurance, completing the priority accreditation applications, hiring the Operations Manager, and issuing the first Gemini sub-contract instruction. Section 02 · Strategic rationale

A clean entity, by design.

The case for a standalone subsidiary rests on three intertwined arguments: protecting Gemini, maximising P&L clarity, and giving the new business the right operating envelope to grow.

2.1 · Why a separate entity

Running building services work inside Gemini AMPM creates four structural problems:

  • Risk contamination. New supply-chain relationships, new accreditation requirements and new client credit exposures would be consolidated onto Gemini's balance sheet and accreditation register — placing the 27-year-old flagship entity at unnecessary risk.
  • P&L clarity. Building-services work mixed into Gemini's accounts would obscure the true performance of both businesses, complicating investor reporting and division-level decision-making.
  • Accreditation separation. Building Services requires different accreditations — NICEIC or NAPIT for electrical, Gas Safe for gas, FMB for general building — that are distinct from Gemini's fire and life-safety stack and should be developed independently.
  • Incentive alignment. A standalone entity supports a separate management incentive structure — critical for recruiting and retaining the operational leadership needed to grow the business.

2.2 · Why now

  • Gemini's existing project pipeline already contains M&E, fit-out, roofing and groundworks scope currently being sub-contracted — captive revenue available on day one
  • Several Tier-1 and institutional clients already known to the management team are actively looking for a reliable direct building-services contractor, reducing new-business development time and cost
  • The building-services market is strong: post-pandemic construction activity, energy-efficiency upgrades (heat pump, EV charging, HVAC retrofits) and Building Safety Act remediation all drive sustained demand
  • Group overheads are already in place — shared back-office, finance, HR and IT functions mean Building Services can launch with minimal additional fixed cost

2.3 · De-risking Gemini — an explicit design principle

The principle

  • Every new supply-chain relationship, new client contract and new accreditation added to AMPM Building Services is one that does not appear on Gemini's risk register.
  • Gemini retains its clean 27-year track record and its mature accreditation stack.
  • Building Services starts fresh — appropriate for an entity that will spend its first 12–18 months establishing itself.
  • If a contract or supplier later proves to be a problem, it is isolated within Building Services and does not threaten the flagship.
Section 03 · Services and scope

Five service lines. M&E first.

AMPM Building Services operates across five service lines. Initial focus is Mechanical and Electrical — the services most heavily sub-contracted by Gemini today and therefore the quickest route to captive revenue. Fit-out, roofing and groundworks scale as the supply chain and accreditation position develops.

Section 04 · Go-to-market strategy

Two phases, running in parallel.

Phase 1 is internal — sub-contract into Gemini AMPM. Phase 2 is external — direct relationships with friendly clients. Both phases are underway simultaneously: Phase 2 begins as soon as insurance and accreditations allow.

Phase 1 · Internal

Sub-contracting into Gemini AMPM

Month 1–3 · ~£1.5–2m year-one contribution

AMPM Building Services is appointed as a sub-contractor on Gemini AMPM projects where M&E, fit-out, roofing or groundworks scope exists. This is a launch mechanism, not a permanent arrangement — Gemini's existing project order book provides sufficient pipeline to generate an initial run-rate while Phase 2 development runs in parallel.

  • Immediate revenue · day-one pipeline without new client development time
  • Operational track record · real project delivery experience before external-facing activity begins
  • Supply-chain development · opportunity to qualify, assess and embed preferred sub-contractors under controlled conditions
  • Cash-flow foundation · Gemini pays Building Services on normal commercial sub-contractor terms
Phase 2 · External

Direct client relationships

Month 2–6 · ~£1.5–2m year-one contribution

Parallel to Phase 1, AMPM Building Services approaches known, friendly clients directly. These are clients already in the Group's or management team's network who are actively procuring building-services work. The management team has specific client conversations already in progress.

  • Existing Gemini clients with separate M&E or fit-out requirements procured elsewhere
  • Tier-1 contractors (Mace, Arc Group) where building-services packages are let separately from fire safety
  • Housing associations and residential property managers with planned maintenance and improvement programmes
  • Commercial landlords with energy-efficiency and fit-out requirements

Target: first direct-client instruction within 60 days of launch.

Section 05 · Accreditations and insurance roadmap

A fresh stack, built fast.

AMPM Building Services must build its own accreditation and insurance position independently of Gemini. The following are required for the entity to trade credibly across all five service lines.

5.1 · Priority accreditations

Accreditation Required for Priority Typical timeline Action
NICEIC or NAPIT Electrical contracting Critical 4–8 weeks Apply immediately · qualified electricians must be on staff or engaged
Gas Safe Registration Gas installation and servicing Critical 2–4 weeks Register qualified Gas Safe engineers on company card
CHAS or Constructionline General contractor pre-qualification High 4–6 weeks Apply · leverages Group's existing Constructionline Gold where possible
FMB General building credibility Medium 4–8 weeks Apply during setup phase
F-Gas certification Refrigeration / air conditioning Medium Via operative cert. Ensure relevant operatives hold F-Gas qualification
ISO 9001 / 45001 Quality and H&S management Medium-term 3–6 months Implement management system; target certification in Year 1

5.2 · Insurance requirements

Before the first instruction is raised, the following must be in place:

  • Employers Liability Insurance — statutory minimum £5m; recommend £10m given scale ambition
  • Public Liability Insurance — £5m minimum; £10m recommended for Tier-1 contractor supply-chain requirements
  • Professional Indemnity Insurance — required for design-and-build and specification work; £1–2m cover
  • Contract Works / All-Risks Insurance — covers works in progress; required for most main-contractor supply-chain appointments
  • Plant and Equipment Insurance — if plant is owned rather than hired

Bridging arrangement for Phase 1

  • Insurance must be placed before Phase 2 (direct client) activity begins.
  • Phase 1 (sub into Gemini) can begin under Gemini's umbrella insurance while AMPM Building Services' own policies are arranged.
  • This is to be confirmed with the Group's insurance broker as a priority action in Week 1.
Section 06 · Year-one financial plan

£4m revenue. 25% GP. 10% OP.

The year-one financial plan is anchored in known channels and conservative assumptions. The £4 million revenue target is built bottom-up from three identifiable channels, with margin discipline from day one and overhead held in check by Group-shared back-office support.

6.1 · Revenue model

  • Gemini sub-contracting channel — £1.5–2m. Conservative estimate based on the M&E and fit-out scope visible in Gemini's current order book and pipeline.
  • Direct client channel · existing network — £1–1.5m. Based on confirmed client conversations and known procurement pipelines where the management team has existing relationships.
  • Direct client channel · new business — £0.5m. Conservative allowance for new tender and framework activity beginning in Q2.

The 25% GP target is achievable on the channel mix: Gemini sub-contracting is set internally at a defensible margin, and direct work is priced at prevailing market rates. The operating profit is sensitive to overhead management — shared Group back-office support is essential to keep overhead below 15% of revenue in Year 1.

6.3 · Key financial assumptions

  • Direct labour — mix of 2–3 employed trade supervisors / project managers plus sub-contracted operative labour, scaling with revenue
  • Premises — no dedicated AMPM Building Services premises required in Year 1; works from Group base with site-based operations
  • Fleet — initial fleet of 2–3 transit vans; additional vehicles hired as revenue ramps
  • Payment terms — 30-day net standard; Gemini channel settled monthly
  • Working capital — modest requirement: sub-contracting channel provides predictable monthly income

7.2 · Supply chain

An early priority is building a qualified, reliable supply chain across all five service lines:

  • Electrical — NICEIC-approved electrical sub-contractors and qualified electricians: 2–3 preferred suppliers to be identified and approved in Month 1
  • Mechanical — plumbing and heating sub-contractors and Gas Safe engineers: existing Gemini supply chain referenced, but formal Building Services agreements must be executed
  • Fit-out — dry-lining and partitioning, joinery and finishing trades: 3–4 preferred sub-contractors
  • Roofing — GRP / EPDM flat roofing and pitched roofing specialists: 2 preferred suppliers minimum
  • Groundworks — drainage and concrete sub-contractors: 2 preferred suppliers

All supply-chain appointments must go through the Group's standard qualification process: PL and EL insurance verification, references, and method statement / RAMS capability check.

7.3 · Systems and reporting

  • Procore (project management) — already in use at Gemini; extend licence to Building Services immediately
  • Xero (financial) — separate entity on Group Xero account; set up before first invoice
  • Uptick (asset management) — relevant for planned maintenance contracts as these develop; not priority Day 1
  • AMPM Live — Building Services clients will be onboarded to the Group's client portal when it launches
