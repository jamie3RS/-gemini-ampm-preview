Section 1

Executive Summary

AMPM Building Services Limited is an incorporated, trading subsidiary of AMPM Group Limited. It is launched as a full building-services contractor across five service lines: mechanical and electrical (M&E), fit-out, fabric, roofing, and groundworks. The entity exists to capture building-services work currently sub-contracted out of Gemini AMPM to third parties — bringing that margin in-house, onto a separate profit-and-loss account, with a clean and clearly governed risk profile.

The strategy is deliberately phased. In Phase 1, AMPM Building Services sub-contracts into Gemini AMPM on live projects, generating immediate revenue with minimal new-business risk. In Phase 2, the entity wins its own direct clients, building an independent order book. The Year-1 revenue target is £4.0 million.

This structure de-risks Gemini AMPM. New supply-chain relationships, new client credit exposures and the operating risks of a fast-growing entity are held in the new company — not consolidated onto the balance sheet and accreditation register of the Group's 27-year-old flagship.

The financial plan is conservative by design. Year 1 is planned as a controlled near-breakeven (operating profit of approximately £54k on £4.0m revenue) as the entity establishes its supply chain, accreditations and direct order book. Margin matures as the higher-value direct channel grows: the plan reaches approximately £10m revenue and 2.8% EBITDA by Year 3.

Section 3

Strategic Rationale

Why a separate entity

Running building-services work inside Gemini AMPM creates four structural problems:

  • Risk contamination New supply chains, new accreditation requirements and new client credit exposures are consolidated onto Gemini's balance sheet and accreditation register, placing the 27-year-old flagship at unnecessary risk.
  • P&L opacity Building-services work mixed into Gemini's accounts obscures the true performance of both businesses, complicating board and investor reporting.
  • Accreditation mismatch Building Services requires different accreditations — NICEIC or NAPIT (electrical), Gas Safe (gas), FMB and trade-specific schemes — distinct from Gemini's fire and life-safety stack.
  • Incentive alignment A standalone entity supports a separate management-incentive structure, critical to recruiting and retaining the operational leadership needed to scale.

Why now

  • Gemini's live pipeline already contains M&E, fit-out, fabric, roofing and groundworks scope being sub-contracted out — immediate, captive revenue.
  • Several Tier-1 and institutional clients known to the management team are actively seeking a reliable direct building-services contractor, shortening new-business development time.
  • Market demand is strong: post-pandemic construction activity, energy-efficiency retrofit (heat pumps, EV charging, HVAC), and Building Safety Act remediation all drive volume.
  • Group overheads — finance, HR, IT, marketing — are already in place, so the entity launches with minimal incremental fixed cost.

De-risking Gemini — the design principle

Every new supply-chain relationship, client contract and accreditation added to AMPM Building Services is one that does not appear on Gemini's risk register. Gemini retains its clean 27-year track record and mature accreditation stack. Building Services starts fresh — appropriate for an entity that will spend its first 12–18 months establishing itself.

Section 5

Market Context

The UK building-services market is supported by three durable demand drivers, each aligned to a service line:

  • Building Safety Act 2022 Remediation of Higher-Risk Buildings and the golden-thread compliance regime drive sustained fabric, façade and M&E demand — an area where the Group's compliance heritage is a direct credibility advantage.
  • Decarbonisation and energy efficiency Heat-pump installation, HVAC retrofit, EWI and EV-charging rollout underpin the M&E and fabric pipelines across commercial and residential stock.
  • Tier-1 supply-chain demand Main contractors continue to let M&E and fit-out packages to capable, compliance-ready sub-contractors — the segment Phase 1 and Phase 2 both target.

Geographically the entity follows the Group: London and the South of England, where the management team's client relationships are concentrated. National reach, where required, is a Group-level matter delivered through acquisition rather than organic stretch.

Section 6

Go-to-Market Strategy

Phase 1 — Internal sub-contracting (Months 1–3)

AMPM Building Services is appointed as a sub-contractor on Gemini projects carrying M&E, fit-out, fabric, roofing or groundworks scope. This delivers:

  • Immediate revenue — a day-one pipeline without new-client development time.
  • Operational track record — real delivery experience before external-facing activity begins.
  • Supply-chain development — the opportunity to qualify and embed preferred sub-contractors under controlled conditions.
  • Cash-flow foundation — Gemini settles on normal commercial sub-contractor terms.

This is a launch mechanism, not a permanent arrangement. Gemini's order book provides sufficient pipeline to establish an initial run-rate while Phase 2 develops in parallel.

Phase 2 — Direct client relationships (Months 2–6)

In parallel, the entity approaches known, friendly clients already procuring building-services work:

  • Existing Gemini fire and life-safety clients with separate M&E or fit-out requirements procured elsewhere.
  • Tier-1 contractors (Mace, Arc Group) where building-services packages are let separately from fire safety.
  • Housing associations and residential property managers with planned-maintenance and improvement programmes.
  • Commercial landlords with energy-efficiency and fit-out requirements.

Specific client conversations are already in progress. Target: first direct client instruction within 60 days of launch.

Insurance requirements

The following must be in place before the first direct instruction is raised:

  • Employers' Liability — statutory minimum £5m; £10m recommended given scale ambition.
  • Public Liability — £5m minimum; £10m recommended for Tier-1 supply-chain requirements.
  • Professional Indemnity — £1–2m, required for design-and-build and specification work.
  • Contract Works / All-Risks — covers works in progress; required for most main-contractor appointments.
  • Plant and Equipment — if plant is owned rather than hired.
Sequencing note

Phase 1 (sub-contracting into Gemini) can begin under Gemini's umbrella insurance while the entity's own policies are arranged; this must be confirmed with the Group broker as a priority. The entity's own cover must be in place before Phase 2 direct activity begins.

Revenue ramp and margin maturity

Year 1 is planned as a controlled near-breakeven. Margin is intentionally thin while the entity is weighted toward the lower-margin Gemini sub-contract channel and is absorbing first-year accreditation and set-up cost. As the higher-margin direct channel grows — from £2.0m in Year 1 to £7.0m by Year 3 — blended gross margin firms and operating margin roughly doubles. These are deliberately conservative contracting margins, not aspirational figures.

Working capital and cash

The Year-1 monthly cash-flow shows a peak working-capital requirement of approximately £157k during the ramp, driven by the lag between cost outlay and client receipts plus retention withheld. The board is asked to approve a ring-fenced facility of up to £200k to cover this with headroom.

Critical control

This facility must be funded independently of Gemini AMPM. Gemini's own cash position is tight, and the explicit design principle of this entity is to remove building-services risk and cash drag from the flagship — not to transfer it. Building Services must be self-funding from its own facility from day one.

Key assumptions

  • Gross margins by channel: Gemini sub-contract 12.5%, direct (existing) 17%, direct (new) 16%.
  • No dedicated premises in Year 1 — operates from the Group base with site-based delivery.
  • Initial fleet of 2–3 vans; additional capacity hired as revenue ramps.
  • Standard 30-day payment terms; Gemini channel settled monthly. 5% retention typical, released in the following year.
  • Employer's NI and pension loaded at 15% on base salaries; group management charge ramps from £60k (Y1) to £150k (Y3).

Operations and Team

Leadership and structure

AMPM Building Services launches under the existing Group leadership. Jamie Dawson (Chair), Dan Small (Managing Director) and Luke Finney (Commercial Director) retain their Group roles in the new company. Steve Lee moves across from Gemini to run the project side of the business. Joe Isaac and Jill Wilson each contribute one day per week alongside their Gemini roles, providing experienced commercial and delivery support without a full-time fixed cost.

Inter-company arrangements

The time of Gemini personnel supporting Building Services is provided under inter-company recharge or secondment agreements, so the cost sits in Building Services and not Gemini. This is a deliberate control: it keeps both profit-and-loss accounts clean and preserves the principle that Gemini does not subsidise the new entity. These agreements are to be executed before launch.

An early priority is a qualified, reliable supply chain across all five lines. All appointments pass the Group's standard qualification: PL/EL insurance verification, references, and RAMS capability check.

Section 12

Relationship to Gemini and the Group

Honest starting position

Building-services activity has been running inside Gemini since January 2026 and has been loss-making from inception — a small revenue line at a heavily negative gross margin, flagged in the Q2 board pack as requiring a decision. This entity is that decision. It moves the scope out of Gemini and into a properly structured, separately managed and separately accredited business with its own P&L, supply chain and commercial control — converting an uncontrolled cost line inside the flagship into a managed, margin-accountable subsidiary.

AMPM Building Services was referenced in the AMPM Group Business Plan (May 2026) as a Year-1 subsidiary. This paper provides the operational and financial detail behind that reference, and confirms the Year-1 revenue contribution of £4.0m to the Group's consolidated plan.

Within the Group architecture, Building Services absorbs the M&E and building-services scope previously procured outside Gemini; Gemini provides the revenue base, accreditation credibility and client introductions; and the entity contributes a third independent revenue stream toward the Group's three-year ambition alongside acquisitions.
