[Heading1] 3.  Market Opportunity
The UK compliance services sector is experiencing a period of regulatory-driven, structural growth. Three converging forces make this an attractive moment to build a scaled platform.
[Heading2] 3.1  Building Safety Act 2022 — Mandatory Compliance Uplift
The Building Safety Act 2022 introduced the most significant change to UK fire and structural safety regulation in decades. Key obligations now enforceable include:
[ListParagraph] Duty holder accountability across design, construction, and occupation phases
[ListParagraph] Golden thread requirement: digital documentation of all safety-critical information maintained throughout the building lifecycle
[ListParagraph] Higher-Risk Building inspection and certification regime managed by the Building Safety Regulator
[ListParagraph] Enhanced Remediation Orders for residential buildings — driving significant passive fire and fire door remediation programmes
Compliance is not optional and not discretionary. Building owners face criminal liability for non-compliance. This drives sustained, non-cyclical demand for accredited contractors with documented golden thread capability — precisely the offering AMPM Group is built around.
[Heading2] 3.2  Market Consolidation — Validation of the Thesis
The consolidation trend validates both the market opportunity and the exit thesis:
[ListParagraph] Marlowe Group has been systematically acquiring fire safety and compliance businesses across the UK, building a £400m+ revenue platform
[ListParagraph] Mitie has made multiple acquisitions in compliance and technical FM, integrating life safety within a broader FM offering
[ListParagraph] Both have acquired direct competitors to Gemini AMPM, demonstrating that assets of this type trade at meaningful multiples to strategic acquirers
[ListParagraph] andwis Group — backed by H.I.G. Capital (€8bn+ AUM European PE firm) — has built a £300m+ technical building services platform comprising 18 companies across five disciplines since incorporation in August 2022. This is the most direct comparable to the AMPM Group model and demonstrates that institutional PE capital can scale a compliant acquisition platform in this sector at significant speed
The participation of H.I.G. Capital, Marlowe Group, and Mitie in this sector represents institutional-level validation that the compliance services roll-up model generates strong returns. The acquirer universe for an exit event in five years is well-established and well-capitalised. AMPM Group at £25–30 million revenue, with a proprietary technology platform and a management team carrying deep operational track record, is a differentiated entry point into that same return thesis.
[Heading2] 3.3  Public Sector Framework Access — EOT Competitive Advantage
PPN 06/20 (Procurement Policy Note: Taking Account of a Supplier's Approach to Carbon Reduction and Social Value) scores social value as a mandatory element of public sector procurement. Employee-owned businesses score materially higher than privately-owned competitors. Gemini AMPM's EOT structure provides a genuine, structural advantage in competitive tendering for:
[ListParagraph] Local authority property portfolios
[ListParagraph] NHS and healthcare estates
[ListParagraph] Education frameworks (universities, academies, maintained schools)
[ListParagraph] Social housing providers and registered social landlords
[Heading2] 3.4  Sector Fragmentation — Acquisition Pipeline
The compliance services market remains highly fragmented below the Marlowe and Mitie tier. There are hundreds of sub-£5 million regional operators with:
[ListParagraph] Loyal client books but no succession plan
[ListParagraph] Complementary accreditations (e.g., gas, water hygiene, lifts)
[ListParagraph] Geographic coverage the Group does not yet hold
[ListParagraph] Key operators whose relationships and contracts represent the real value
This fragmentation creates a favourable acquisition environment. Vendors in the £1–5 million revenue range typically trade at 3–5x EBITDA, creating meaningful value creation opportunity when integrated into a platform business that should command 6–8x on exit.
[Heading2] 3.5  Competitive Landscape — AMPM’s Differentiated Position
The compliance and technical building services market has three tiers of competitor. Understanding where AMPM Group sits relative to each tier is essential context for evaluating the investment proposition.
Tier 1 — Large-Cap Roll-Ups: Marlowe Group and Mitie
Marlowe Group (£400m+ revenue) and Mitie operate at scale beyond AMPM’s near-term target range. Both are listed or institutional-backed acquirers operating national frameworks. They validate the exit thesis — they are the natural buyers of a scaled AMPM Group at the end of the hold period — but they are not direct competitors at the contract level. Their minimum acquisition interest begins at revenue levels AMPM is targeting as a 5-year destination, not a starting point.
Tier 2 — Mid-Market Roll-Up: andwis Group
andwis Group is the most directly comparable platform to AMPM. Incorporated in August 2022 and backed by H.I.G. Capital (€8bn+ AUM), andwis has assembled 18 operating companies across five service divisions — fire and security, environmental services, lifts and entrance, maintenance and response, and mechanical and electrical — generating £300m+ revenue from 25,000 customers with 2,200+ employees. Credit data (Experian, May 2026) confirms H.I.G. as the ultimate holding entity, with Ares Management and Barclays providing the debt stack. Long-term liabilities at HoldCo level reached £112.7m at December 2024, with £17.8m of annual interest cost — a heavily leveraged structure typical of aggressive PE-backed acquisition programmes.
andwis serves as the strongest possible proof of concept for the AMPM Group model: an institutional PE firm deployed significant capital into a technical building services consolidation play in the same market, with the same acquisition-led approach, in the same period. That it reached £300m+ in under three years demonstrates both the availability of acquisition targets and the appetite of strategic buyers at scale.
AMPM Group and andwis are differentiated on four dimensions:
[ListParagraph] Sector focus. andwis targets technical building services broadly — lifts, doors, asbestos, M&E. AMPM Group is focused on the compliance-first, fire safety and life-safety segment with an explicit Building Safety Act positioning and residential/housing FM adjacency. These are related but distinct market positions.
[ListParagraph] Technology layer. andwis has no visible client-facing technology platform. AMPM Live — a proprietary client portal providing live asset registers, compliance dashboards, and golden thread documentation — creates structural client stickiness and positions AMPM Group as a software-enabled services business, not a pure trade contractor. This distinction supports a premium valuation multiple on exit.
[ListParagraph] Capital structure. andwis carries £112.7m of long-term liabilities at HoldCo level and a debt gearing ratio of 877% (Experian, December 2024). AMPM Group’s investment ask of £10m acquisition facility plus £1m working capital is deliberately conservative — sized to generate strong risk-adjusted returns without the fragility of a heavily leveraged structure. This is not a weakness in ambition; it is a more resilient model for an investor seeking capital preservation alongside growth.
[ListParagraph] Operator-led management. andwis was assembled by a PE firm and run by an installed management team. AMPM Group is led by the founders who built, ran, and sold the core asset over 27 years. Client relationships, accreditation credibility, and sector knowledge are intrinsic to the management team — not acquired alongside a business. This is a structural difference in execution risk.
andwis is also a credible addition to the exit acquirer pool. At their current acquisition pace, they will require more London and South of England fire and life-safety capacity. A scaled AMPM Group — with accreditation depth, a proprietary portal, and an established client base — is precisely the type of platform they acquire.
Tier 3 — Local Independents
Below the mid-market roll-ups sit hundreds of sub-£5m regional operators. These are not strategic threats — Gemini AMPM has consistently won against them on quality, accreditation depth, and client trust. They are, however, the primary acquisition targets for AMPM Ventures. Their value is in their client books, their geographic coverage, and in some cases their operators — not in their balance sheets or systems.

[Heading1] 8.  Technology — AMPM Live
AMPM Live is a client-facing digital platform that provides building owners and facilities managers with real-time visibility of their assets, compliance status, and service delivery. It is designed to move AMPM Group from a service provider to a strategic infrastructure partner — central to how clients manage their compliance obligations.
[Heading2] 8.1  Platform Overview
AMPM Live is a multi-user portal with role-based access. Each client's view is configured to their specific asset base and reporting requirements:
[ListParagraph] Live asset register — every asset on site, with status, last service date, and next due date
[ListParagraph] Compliance dashboard — real-time visibility of outstanding actions, overdue items, and certification status
[ListParagraph] KPI reporting — customisable metrics relevant to the client's obligations (fire door inspections, detection system tests, suppression certifications, etc.)
[ListParagraph] Job instruction — clients can raise reactive jobs, request quotations, or approve variations directly through the portal
[ListParagraph] Document repository — all certificates, inspection reports, and remediation records stored and accessible on demand, meeting golden thread obligations under the Building Safety Act
[Heading2] 8.2  Why This Matters Commercially
The portal creates structural switching cost and deepens the client relationship:
[ListParagraph] Once a client's asset base is onboarded to AMPM Live, the data relationship makes switching to a competitor operationally disruptive
[ListParagraph] It positions AMPM as a technology-enabled partner, not a commodity trades contractor — supporting premium pricing
[ListParagraph] It enables proactive compliance management rather than reactive break-fix, driving higher annual contract values
[ListParagraph] Golden thread documentation within the platform directly addresses the Building Safety Act's digital record-keeping requirement, making AMPM Live a compliance tool as well as a client service tool
[Heading2] 8.3  Technology Investment
AMPM Live is in active development. A portion of the Group working capital fund is earmarked for platform build. The target is soft launch to existing Gemini clients in 2026, with full commercial release as a value-added service layer in 2027.

[Heading2] 9.2  AMPM Academy
AMPM Academy is a planned Group entity targeted for launch in Q3 2027. It addresses a problem that is both personal and commercial: the UK compliance and building services sector has a chronic skills pipeline problem, and not enough opportunity is being created for young people entering the trades.
The Academy has three commercial purposes:
[ListParagraph] Internal pipeline: apprentices trained to Group standards and absorbed into Gemini and the subsidiary businesses, reducing reliance on the external labour market and stabilising wage costs at scale
[ListParagraph] Workforce differentiation: a trained, culturally-aligned workforce creates a service quality advantage over competitors relying on labour agencies and subcontract labour
[ListParagraph] External IP licensing: accreditation pathways, training content, and regulatory compliance curricula licensed to other operators in the sector — a recurring, high-margin revenue stream that does not require headcount growth
Apprenticeship programmes are already running within Gemini AMPM at small scale. The Academy formalises and expands this, with a dedicated entity structure and commercial development plan.
This is also a material asset in PE exit positioning. An owned training and accreditation platform adds demonstrable scarcity value to an acquirer seeking to inherit a sustainable talent pipeline, not just a client book.
[Heading2] 9.3  Employee Ownership Trust Heritage
The EOT transition at Gemini AMPM (October 2024) reflected the founders' commitment to the workforce that built the business. The EOT structure is preserved within the Group architecture. Beyond its governance implications, it creates:
[ListParagraph] A cultural alignment between the Group's values and its people — reduced turnover, higher discretionary effort
[ListParagraph] A public sector procurement advantage under PPN 06/20 social value scoring
[ListParagraph] A narrative that differentiates the Group from corporate roll-up acquirers in client conversations
