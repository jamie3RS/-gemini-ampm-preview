[P]ENTITY STATUS
[P]
[P]AMPM Building Services Limited is an incorporated subsidiary of AMPM Group Limited. It is being launched as a full building services contractor covering mechanical and electrical (M&E), fit-out, roofing, and groundworks. The entity is designed to capture a large volume of building services work currently being sub-contracted out of Gemini AMPM to third parties — bringing it in-house, on a separate P&L, with a clean risk profile.
[P]The strategy is phased. In Phase 1, AMPM Building Services sub-contracts into Gemini AMPM on existing projects, generating immediate revenue with low new-business risk. In Phase 2, the entity approaches friendly clients directly, establishing its own client relationships and order book. The year-one revenue target is £4 million.
[P]This structure deliberately de-risks Gemini AMPM: new supply chain relationships, new client contracts, and the operational risks inherent in a growing business entity are held within the new company rather than consolidated onto Gemini's P&L and accreditation register.
[P]
[P]AMPM Building Services is the Group's fastest path to additional revenue in the near term. It is not a speculative future entity — it is operationally ready and has an identified pipeline from day one via the Gemini relationship.
[P]
[P]
[P]
[P]2.  Strategic Rationale
[P]
[P]2.1  Why a Separate Entity
[P]Running building services work within Gemini AMPM creates several structural problems:
[P]
[P]Risk contamination: new supply chain relationships, new accreditation requirements, and new client credit exposures are consolidated onto Gemini's balance sheet and accreditation register — placing the 27-year-old flagship entity at unnecessary risk
[P]
[P]P&L clarity: building services work mixed into Gemini's accounts obscures the true performance of both businesses, complicating investor reporting and division-level decision-making
[P]
[P]Accreditation separation: Building Services will require different accreditations (NICEIC or NAPIT for electrical, Gas Safe for gas, FMB for general building) that are distinct from Gemini's fire and life-safety accreditation stack
[P]
[P]Incentive alignment: a standalone entity allows for a separate management incentive structure — critical for recruiting and retaining the operational leadership needed to grow the business
[P]
[P]
[P]2.2  Why Now
[P]
[P]Gemini's existing project pipeline contains M&E, fit-out, roofing, and groundworks scope currently being sub-contracted — this represents immediate, captive revenue
[P]
[P]Several Tier 1 and institutional clients already known to the management team are actively looking for a reliable direct building services contractor, reducing new-business development time and cost
[P]
[P]The building services market is strong: post-pandemic construction activity, energy efficiency upgrades (heat pump, EV charging, HVAC retrofits), and Building Safety Act remediation all drive demand
[P]
[P]The Group's overheads are already in place — shared back-office, finance, HR and IT functions mean Building Services can launch with minimal additional fixed cost
[P]
[P]
[P]2.3  De-risking Gemini
[P]This is an explicit design principle. Every new supply chain relationship, new client contract, and new accreditation added to AMPM Building Services is one that does not appear on Gemini's risk register. Gemini retains its clean 27-year track record and its mature accreditation stack. Building Services starts fresh — which is appropriate for an entity that will spend its first 12–18 months establishing itself.
[P]

[P]4.  Go-to-Market Strategy
[P]Two-phase model: Phase 1 is internal (sub into Gemini). Phase 2 is external (direct client relationships). Both phases are underway simultaneously — Phase 2 begins as soon as insurance and accreditations allow.
[P]
[P]
[P]Phase 1 — Internal Sub-contracting (Month 1–3)
[P]AMPM Building Services is appointed as a sub-contractor on Gemini AMPM projects where M&E, fit-out, roofing, or groundworks scope exists. This delivers:
[P]
[P]Immediate revenue: day one pipeline without new client development time
[P]
[P]Operational track record: real project delivery experience before external-facing activity begins
[P]
[P]Supply chain development: opportunity to qualify, assess, and embed preferred sub-contractors under controlled conditions
[P]
[P]Cash flow foundation: Gemini pays AMPM Building Services on normal commercial sub-contractor terms
[P]
[P]This is not a permanent arrangement — it is a launch mechanism. Gemini's existing project order book provides sufficient pipeline to generate an initial run-rate while Phase 2 development runs in parallel.
[P]
[P]
[P]Phase 2 — Direct Client Relationships (Month 2–6)
[P]Parallel to Phase 1, AMPM Building Services approaches known, friendly clients directly. These are clients already in the Group's or management team's network who are actively procuring building services work:
[P]
[P]Clients currently using Gemini for fire and life-safety, who have separate M&E or fit-out requirements procured elsewhere
[P]
[P]Tier 1 contractors (Mace, Arc Group) where building services packages are let separately from fire safety
[P]
[P]Housing associations and residential property managers with planned maintenance and improvement programmes
[P]
[P]Commercial landlords with energy efficiency and fit-out requirements
[P]
[P]The management team has specific client conversations already in progress. Target: first direct client instruction within 60 days of launch.

[P]Insurance must be placed before Phase 2 client activity begins. Phase 1 (sub into Gemini) can begin under Gemini's umbrella insurance while AMPM Building Services' own policies are arranged — this should be confirmed with the Group's insurance broker as a priority action.
